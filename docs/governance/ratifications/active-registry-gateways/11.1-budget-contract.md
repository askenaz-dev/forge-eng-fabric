# Ratification 11.1 — Budget Contract Per-Family Categorization

**Gate for:** Release N · **Spec:** [active-registry-gateways/tasks.md §11.1](../../../../openspec/changes/active-registry-gateways/tasks.md)
**Status:** Open · **Owner:** Platform Engineering + LiteLLM stewards
**Decision required by:** Release N freeze date

## The question

> Does the existing LiteLLM Tenant-budget endpoint expose enough for non-LLM (MCP / A2A) cost categorization, or do we introduce per-asset-family cost categories on it?

## Why it matters

`mcp-gateway` and `a2a-gateway` consult the budget service before every dispatch. If the budget service is LLM-only, the new gateways will need to either:
(a) duplicate budgeting in-process — drift between LiteLLM and the new gateways becomes inevitable;
(b) skip budgeting — production traffic ungoverned;
(c) get extended with per-family categories.

Option (c) is the design's stated intent ([design.md decision 8](../../../../openspec/changes/active-registry-gateways/design.md)).

## Proposed decision

Adopt the canonical [`contracts/openapi/tenant-budget.yaml`](../../../../contracts/openapi/tenant-budget.yaml) (`family ∈ {llm, mcp, a2a}`) as the single source of truth. Migration order:

1. `model-gateway` (LiteLLM) starts setting `family=llm` on every probe. Today's calls without the field are treated as `family=llm` for backwards compatibility — no behavior change.
2. `mcp-gateway` ships in Release N with `family=mcp`.
3. `a2a-gateway` ships in Release N with `family=a2a`.
4. The tenant-budget service exposes per-family limits in its config; default policy mirrors today's LLM-only behavior so no existing Tenant gets stricter.

## Implementation status

- Contract: [contracts/openapi/tenant-budget.yaml](../../../../contracts/openapi/tenant-budget.yaml) — landed.
- Service: tenant-budget service does not exist yet as a separate component; the LiteLLM-internal budget tracking handles LLM today. A standalone service is on the roadmap; until it lands the new gateways accept a configurable `BUDGET_URL` and gracefully fail-open when unset (`reason=budget_unconfigured`).
- New gateway behavior under "unconfigured": permit the call, emit an audit reason indicating the fail-open. Operators get a visible signal in Grafana before the service is up.

## Open follow-ups

- The standalone `tenant-budget` service ships in a separate spec (out of scope here). When it lands, the gateways switch from `BUDGET_URL=""` (fail-open) to `BUDGET_URL=http://tenant-budget:8080` and the **MCPGatewayBudgetBlocksSpike** alert starts having signal.
- Confirm with LiteLLM stewards that the contract's `family` enum doesn't conflict with any LiteLLM-internal model-routing labels.
